## Abstract

Designing controllers with provable formal guarantees has become an urgent requirement for cyber-physical systems in safety-critical scenarios. 
In this paper, by leveraging feedback linearization and backstepping techniques, we present a novel framework for constructing **provable 
reach-avoid** formal certificates tailored to multi-input multi-output systems. Based on this, we developed a systematic synthesis approach for 
controllers with reach-avoid guarantees, which ensures that the outputs of the system eventually enter the predefined target set while staying 
within the required safe set. Finally, we validate our method in numerical simulations.

## Experiments

### Dubins Car

Consider the following modified Dubins car model:

$$
\dot{\bm{x}} =
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{x}_1 \\
\dot{x}_2 \\
\dot{\theta} \\
\dot{v}
\end{bmatrix}
=
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
v\cos\theta \\
v\sin\theta \\
0 \\
0
\end{bmatrix}}_{f(\bm{x})}
+
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 \\
0 & 0 \\
1 & 0 \\
0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}}_{g(\bm{x})}
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
\omega \\
a
\end{bmatrix}}_{\bm{u}}
$$

with $\bm{x} = [x_1, x_2, \theta, v]^\top \in \mathbb{R}^4$, where $(x_1, x_2)$ denotes the planar location, $\theta$ is the heading angle, and $v$ is the forward velocity. The control input is $\bm{u} = [\omega, a]^\top \in \mathbb{R}^2$, where $\omega$ is angular velocity and $a$ is forward acceleration. The system has vector relative degree $\{2,2\}$ with output $\bm{y} = [y_1, y_2]^\top = [x_1, x_2]^\top$.

$$
	\text{Safe set: } \mathcal{C} = \left\{ \bm{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \mid 2(3.5 - y_2(\bm{x}))^2 - 1.5y_1(\bm{x}) - \left(y_1(\bm{x})^4 + y_2(\bm{x})^4 - 3.5^2\right)^2 > 0 \right\}
$$

$$
  \text{Target set: } \mathcal{X}^r = \left\{ \bm{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2 \mid (y_1(\bm{x}) + 0.5)^2 + 4(y_2(\bm{x}) + 1.9)^2 < 1 \right\}
$$

### Two-link Planar Robotic Arm

Consider the following two-link planar robotic arm:

$$
\dot{\bm{q}} =
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{q}_1 \\
\dot{q}_2 \\
-\bm{M}^{-1}(\bm{C}\dot{\bm{q}}+\bm{G})
\end{bmatrix}}_{f(\bm{q})}
+
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
\bm{0}^{2\times2} \\ \bm{M}^{-1}
\end{bmatrix}}_{g(\bm{q})}
\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix}
\tau_1 \\ \tau_2
\end{bmatrix}}_{\bm{u}}
$$

with $\bm{q} = [q_1, q_2, \dot{q}_1, \dot{q}_2]^\top \in \mathbb{R}^4$, where $[q_1, q_2]^\top$ are the joint angles and $[\dot{q}_1, \dot{q}_2]^\top$ are the joint velocities. The inertia matrix $\bm{M}$ is defined as

$$
\bm{M} = \begin{bmatrix} M_{11} & M_{12} \\ M_{21} & M_{22} \end{bmatrix}
$$

where $M_{11} = I_1 + I_2 + m_2(l_1^2 + l_{c_2}^2 + 2l_1 l_{c_2}\cos(q_2)) + m_1 l_{c_1}^2$, $M_{12} = M_{21} = I_2 + m_2(l_{c_2}^2 + l_1 l_{c_2}\cos(q_2))$, $M_{22} = m_2 l_{c_2}^2 + I_2$.

$$
\bm{C} = \begin{bmatrix}
-m_2 l_1 l_{c_2} \sin(q_2)\, \dot{q}_2 & -m_2 l_1 l_{c_2} \sin(q_2)(\dot{q}_1 + \dot{q}_2) \\
m_2 l_1 l_{c_2} \sin(q_2)\, \dot{q}_1 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

is the Coriolis matrix. The gravity vector is

$$
\bm{G} = \begin{bmatrix}
(m_1 g l_{c_1} + m_2 g l_1)\cos(q_1) + m_2 g l_{c_2}\cos(q_1 + q_2) \\
m_2 g l_{c_2}\cos(q_1 + q_2)
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$\bm{\tau} \in \mathbb{R}^2$ is the joint torque as control input. The parameters are listed in the table below.

| $m_1$ | $m_2$ | $l_1$ | $l_2$ | $l_{c_1}$ | $l_{c_2}$ | $I_1$ | $I_2$ | $g$ |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1.0 | 1.0 | 4.0 | 4.0 | 2.0 | 2.0 | 0.02 | 0.02 | 9.81 |

The system has a vector relative degree $\{2,2\}$ with the output $\bm{y} \in \mathbb{R}^2$ as the endpoint position:

$$
\bm{y} = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
l_1\cos(q_1) + l_2\cos(q_1 + q_2) \\
l_1\sin(q_1) + l_2\sin(q_1 + q_2)
\end{bmatrix}.
$$

$$
\text{Safe set: } \mathcal{C} = \lbrace \bm{q} \in \mathbb{R}^4 \mid -(4(y_1(\bm{q})-2) - 2y_2(\bm{q})^3)^2 + 0.8y_2(\bm{q})^3 + 10 > 0 \rbrace
$$

$$
\text{Target set: } \mathcal{X}^r = \lbrace \bm{q} \in \mathbb{R}^4 \mid \tfrac{(y_1(\bm{q})-5.8)^2}{1.2^2} + \tfrac{(y_2(\bm{q})-1.9)^2}{0.4^2} - 1 < 0 \rbrace
$$
